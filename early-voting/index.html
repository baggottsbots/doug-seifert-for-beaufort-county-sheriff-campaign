Simple Steps

How Early Voting Works

Early voting works just like Election Day, with more days and locations to fit your schedule. Here is all it takes.

Step 01

Check Your Registration

Confirm you are registered at your current Beaufort County address before you head out to vote.

Step 02

Pick a Location

Choose whichever of the four early voting centers is most convenient for you. Any location works.

Step 03

Go on a Weekday

Visit any time between 8:30 AM and 5:00 PM, Monday through Friday, from May 26 through June 5.

Step 04

Bring Your Photo ID

Present a valid Photo ID, cast your ballot for Doug Seifert, and you are done. No excuse needed.

Why Vote Early?

Early voting gives every Beaufort County resident more ways to make their voice heard in this Sheriff's race. Life is busy, and Election Day does not always fit a work schedule, a family commitment, or a travel plan.

Voting early means you beat the Election Day lines, lock in your support for Doug Seifert, and never have to worry about an unexpected conflict on June 9 keeping you from the polls.

What to Know Before You Go

  • No excuse or reason is required to vote early.
  • You may vote at any of the four county early voting centers.
  • Early voting centers are open weekdays only, 8:30 AM to 5:00 PM.
  • Centers are closed on weekends and legal holidays.
  • Bring a valid Photo ID, the same as you would on Election Day.
  • If you cannot vote early, polls are open 7:00 AM to 7:00 PM on June 9.
Questions Answered

Early Voting FAQ

Common questions about early voting in the Beaufort County Sheriff primary.

Early in-person voting for the Beaufort County Republican Primary runs Tuesday, May 26 through Friday, June 5, 2026. Early voting centers are open weekdays from 8:30 AM to 5:00 PM and are closed on weekends and holidays.

There are four early voting locations in Beaufort County: the Main Voter Registration Office at 15 John Galt Road in Beaufort, the Bluffton Branch Library at 120 Palmetto Way in Bluffton, the USCB Hilton Head Campus Room 115 at 1 Sand Shark Drive on Hilton Head Island, and the St. Helena Branch Library at 6355 Jonathan Francis Senior Road on St. Helena Island. You may vote at any of the four.

Bring a valid Photo ID. Early voting works just like Election Day, except there are more days and locations to choose from. No excuse is needed to vote early.

The Republican Primary is Tuesday, June 9, 2026. Polls are open from 7:00 AM to 7:00 PM on Election Day. If you cannot make it to an early voting center, you can still vote at your assigned precinct on June 9.
