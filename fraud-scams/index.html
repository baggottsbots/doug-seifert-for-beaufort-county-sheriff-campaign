Beaufort County Sheriff · Fraud & Scam Prevention

Protecting Beaufort County
From Fraud & Scams

Scammers have gotten slick. Doug Seifert has a plan to stop them.

They hide behind the names and ranks of real officers, use your personal information, and manufacture panic to push you into acting before you can think it through. As a former investigator, Doug knows exactly how these crimes work, and how to fight back.

Hear Doug on Fraud & Scams

From Doug

Twenty Years Protecting This Community

I’ve had the pleasure of working for the Beaufort County Sheriff’s Office for the past twenty years. I know this community well. Violent crime, drug-related crime, and traffic safety are real concerns for our residents, and I am committed to addressing each of them fully and effectively.

But as a former investigator, I’m also keenly aware of a threat that’s growing fast: fraud, scams, and internet crime. Over the years, scammers have become far more convincing, using the names and ranks of local law enforcement, your personal identifying information, and a false sense of urgency to force victims into acting immediately. These crimes are costing Beaufort County residents tens of thousands of dollars.

“If you’re scamming people in this county, your time is up.”
The Plan

How Doug Fights Back

Smarter Than the Scammers

Put the latest artificial intelligence fraud detection systems to work, scanning the web to catch scams before they ever reach you, backed by a 24/7 fraud hotline so help is always a call away.

Stronger Together

Forge partnerships with state and federal agencies through memorandums of agreement to expand our investigative reach and ability.

Educate & Prevent

Preventing a scam before it happens is paramount. Our office will keep informing the public on scam types, warning signs, and prevention steps through social media and community events.

Crypto ATM Warnings

Work with County Council to back a local ordinance requiring warning labels on every crypto and Bitcoin ATM, modeled on Omaha, Nebraska Ordinance No. 44007, so folks know the risk before they feed in a dollar.

Stay Alert

Watch Out For

Red Flags of a Scam
  • Unsolicited calls claiming to be from law enforcement or a federal agency
  • Pressure to act immediately, before you can think it through
  • Callers who already have some of your personal information
  • Demands for payment by gift card, wire transfer, or cryptocurrency
  • Anyone telling you to keep the call a secret
In the News

It’s Happening Here

Local Report

An 82-year-old Bluffton man was recently targeted by a scammer posing as a U.S. Marshal, who used the last four digits of his Social Security number to seem legitimate. Bluffton police say these “fictitious allegation” scams, with criminals impersonating law enforcement to scare residents into sending money, are routinely draining thousands from people across the Hilton Head Island and Beaufort County area.
