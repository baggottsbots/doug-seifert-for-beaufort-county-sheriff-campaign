Hear Doug on Fraud & Scams

A Message From Doug Seifert

In His Own Words

I have had the pleasure of working for the Beaufort County Sheriff’s Office for the past twenty years. I know this community well. I know that violent crime, drug-related crime, and traffic safety are major concerns for our residents, and I am committed to addressing each of these issues fully and effectively.

However, as a former investigator, I am also fully aware of the growing trend of fraud, scam, and internet crimes. I know that scammers have become more convincing over the years through their use of local law enforcement names and ranks, personal identifying information, or creating a false sense of urgency requiring victims to take immediate action. These frauds, scams, and internet crimes are costing Beaufort County residents tens of thousands of dollars.

When elected as your Beaufort County Sheriff, I will foster relationships through the use of memorandums of agreement with state and federal law enforcement agencies to help expand our investigative reach and ability.

Preventing an individual from being scammed is paramount. Therefore, I will ensure that the Beaufort County Sheriff’s Office continues to educate and inform the general public of scam types, warning signs, and prevention steps through the use of social media and educational events.

Finally, I plan to work with and encourage County Council to support a local ordinance that would place a warning sticker on all crypto or Bitcoin ATMs, providing notice to customers and users of the potential fraud or scam risks associated with these types of machines. A good example is the Omaha, Nebraska City Ordinance No. 44007, which requires warning labels.

When elected as your Beaufort County Sheriff, I am looking forward to the opportunity to hold people accountable who are taking advantage of our citizens, residents, and guests.

The Strength Already Inside Our Sheriff’s Office

I also want to take this opportunity to educate the public about all the great things our Sheriff’s Office currently has in place. The Sheriff’s Office runs its own forensic service lab for DNA, a Real Time Crime Center, a Drone program, an Air Wing, a Violent Crime and Drug Task Force, multiple K9 units (bloodhounds, bomb dog, narcotic K9, cadaver K9), a Bomb team, SWAT team, Dive team, Marine Patrol, Crime Scene unit, Victim Advocate program, Reserve Deputy program, Crisis Negotiations team, Emergency Management team, Traffic Management Center, 911 operations center, an in-house Chief Financial Officer, an in-house legal team, and a Public Affairs office that oversees both social media and Nixle, and so much more.

Where I Will Build and Improve

This agency also has areas where I plan to immediately build upon and improve. First, I will use current personnel to create a community engagement team to ensure the Beaufort County Sheriff’s Office is consistently engaged in the community. I will engage with County Council to stress the importance of cost-of-living increases so we can retain and recruit quality men and women to Beaufort County. I will encourage municipal and county leaders to slow growth and build up infrastructure to help ease the congestion that frustrates our residents on a daily basis.

I will put a greater emphasis on traffic enforcement by dedicating Road Patrol Deputies whose sole mission is to enforce traffic laws. I plan to expand the Violent Crime and Drug Task Force already in operation by dedicating additional personnel and partnerships with municipal, state, and federal agencies. I will ensure BCSO is more transparent and communicative with the public. When a major incident occurs, I will notify the public via Nixle immediately, hold press conferences as soon as possible, and continue to update the public through additional press conferences, social media, and Nixle.

I will be proud to lead these men and women as the next Sheriff of Beaufort County, and to continue moving our agency forward with experience, integrity, and professional leadership.

An 82-year-old Bluffton man was recently targeted by a scammer posing as a U.S. Marshal, who used the last four digits of his Social Security number to seem legitimate. Bluffton police say these “fictitious allegation” scams, with criminals impersonating law enforcement to scare residents into sending money, are routinely draining thousands from people across the Hilton Head Island and Beaufort County area.
